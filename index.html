The Story

In Pennsylvania, a stranger can walk into Orphans' Court, file a petition claiming to be a "proposed trustee," and walk out with control of your family's trust assets.

No hearing. No notice to all beneficiaries. No verification that the trust is even vacant. Just a judge's signature and—like magic—your inheritance belongs to someone else.

This isn't a hypothetical. This actually happened.

Case Background

Trust: Mitchell E. and Karlie M. Roth Irrevocable Trust

Court: Delaware County Orphans' Court, Pennsylvania

Appellate Docket: No. 1409 EDA 2024 (Superior Court of Pennsylvania)

Supreme Court Docket: PAA Filed — Eastern District

Core Issue: Orphans' Court orders issued without jurisdiction—failure to join indispensable parties (all 10 trust beneficiaries) as required by Pa.R.C.P. 2227(b)

"Ten heirs. One notice. The other nine beneficiaries—including the actual trust beneficiaries—never received notice. The court proceeded anyway. The result: a stranger now controls the trust assets."
