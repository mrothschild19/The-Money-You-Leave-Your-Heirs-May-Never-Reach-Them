Motion to President Judge Wheatcraft to Vacate All Judge Verwey Orders and Reassign Cases

Filed: November 23, 2025 | Chester County Court of Common Pleas | Case No. 2025-06422-PL
To: President Judge Wheatcraft, Chester County Court of Common Pleas

Summary of Relief Requested

  1. Reassign all civil actions brought by Plaintiff assigned to Judge Verwey to a neutral judge of the Chester County Court of Common Pleas.
  2. Vacate all orders, judgments, and rulings entered by Judge Verwey—including the judgment of non-pros, the sanctions order, and the dismissal of Linnea Roth's claims—on the ground that they are void ab initio (42 Pa.C.S. §§ 322(b)-(c); Pa.R.J.A. § 1901).
  3. Award reasonable attorney's fees and costs incurred by Plaintiff (and, where appropriate, to the co-defendant) (Rule 11, Pa.R.Civ.P. § 1032(b)).
  4. Impose monetary sanctions on Defendant Edward L. Perkins, Esq.: treble damages under the Dragonetti Act and statutory elder-abuse damages under 20 Pa.C.S. § 3501.
  5. Issue a permanent injunction prohibiting Defendant Perkins from filing any further pleadings, motions, or other filings in this matter (Rule 12(b)(6)).
  6. Enter a formal reprimand against Defendant Perkins in the Pennsylvania Attorney Disciplinary Board's records for violations of Rule 3.1 and Rule 8.4(c).
  7. Vacate the erroneous dismissal of the co-defendant who had no involvement with the Certificate of Merit or the Notice of Intent, and award that co-defendant reasonable attorney's fees and costs.
  8. Impose monetary sanctions on the Court for the errors, as authorized by Rule 11, In re Mazzoni, Bradley v. United States, and In re Barth.

Key Legal Arguments

The motion was brought pursuant to 42 Pa.C.S. §§ 322(b)-(c) and Pa.R.J.A. § 1901, seeking reassignment based on the appearance of bias demonstrated by Judge Verwey's statements and conduct, coupled with procedural defects that render continued assignment untenable.

"The appearance of bias demonstrated by Judge Verwey's statements and conduct, coupled with the procedural defects identified herein, renders continued assignment to Judge Verwey untenable. An order of reassignment will preserve the integrity of the judicial process, conserve appellate resources and ensure that the parties receive a fair and impartial hearing."

Emergency Matter – Blocked Filing Ability in Case 444-2023-OC

April 13, 2026 | Delaware County Court of Common Pleas
To: Honorable President Judge Cartisano, Court of Common Pleas, Delaware County, Pennsylvania
EMERGENCY: The writer has been BLOCKED from filing any motions in Case 444-2023-OC without explanation, court order, or due process.

I. The Emergency

On or about August 7, 2025, the writer attempted to file a motion in the above-referenced matter. Despite being a pro se litigant with standing as a beneficiary of the Mitchell E. and Karlie M. Roth Irrevocable Trust, she was informed by the Supervisor of the Clerks in Orphans Court in Delaware County that she is unable to file any motions in this case. No explanation was provided. No court order authorized this restriction. No due process was afforded.

This obstruction prevents:

  • Filing motions to vacate void orders
  • Seeking emergency injunctive relief
  • Protecting beneficial interests in the Trust
  • Participating in litigation that directly affects property rights

II. The Underlying Crisis: Tainted Orders Left Standing

"A recused judge's orders are not automatically vacated upon recusal, yet such orders may be fundamentally tainted by the very bias or conflict that necessitated recusal."

Under Pennsylvania law:

  • Orders entered by a judge who should have been disqualified are voidable and may be challenged through appropriate motions
  • When a judge recuses due to actual bias, conflict, or appearance of impropriety, orders issued under that cloud of partiality undermine public confidence in the judiciary
  • Due process requires that litigants have a meaningful opportunity to challenge orders entered by a judge whose impartiality is compromised

The Pennsylvania Supreme Court has recognized that judicial disqualification goes to the integrity of the judicial process itself. In re M.R., 637 Pa. 505, 153 A.3d 1005 (2017).

III. The Harm

The orders left standing by the recused judge have caused, and continue to cause, irreparable harm:

  • Fraudulent trustee appointments remain in effect
  • Trust assets remain under the unlawful control of the Office of Judicial Support who should never have been appointed
  • Beneficiaries' rights are being violated daily
  • The administration of the Mitchell E. and Karlie M. Roth Irrevocable Trust has been corrupted
"I am legally entitled to challenge these orders. Yet I am blocked from doing so while my disabled brother is suffering from malnutrition and liver disease and needs access to the funds his parents left him."

IV. The Relief Requested

  1. Immediately investigate and rectify the block on filing ability in matter 444-2023-OC, ensuring access to the court system to protect rights;
  2. Review the orders entered by the recused judge to determine whether they should be vacated or reconsidered by a neutral jurist;
  3. Consider the appointment of a special master or neutral judge to review the entire matter given the procedural irregularities; and
  4. Take whatever administrative action is necessary to restore access to justice and ensure the integrity of these proceedings.

V. Conclusion

"The right of access to courts is a fundamental constitutional guarantee. Bounds v. Smith, 430 U.S. 817 (1977). Being blocked from filing motions—without explanation, without authority, and without recourse—violates that right. That this obstruction prevents me from challenging orders entered by a now-recused judge only compounds the injustice."

"I am not asking for special treatment. I am asking for what every litigant is entitled to: meaningful access to the courts and a fair hearing before an impartial judge."

Mitchell E. and Karlie M. Roth Irrevocable Trust | Delaware County, PA
