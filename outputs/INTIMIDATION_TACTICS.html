Attorney Intimidation Tactics

When legal arguments fail, some attorneys resort to intimidation.

The following email exchange documents how Attorney Walter Tinsley responded to a pro se litigant's legitimate efforts to mitigate damages—with threats, condescension, and an explicit admission that she is "playing in a game with professionals both lawyers and judges."

"You are an amateur playing in a game with professionals both lawyers and judges. Just wasting our time."
— Attorney Walter Tinsley, August 1, 2025

What This Reveals

This statement is deeply troubling for several reasons:

  • It suggests collusion: If lawyers and judges are "on the same team" against pro se litigants, where is the impartial justice?
  • It admits the system is a "game": Justice should not be a game where professionals exploit amateurs
  • It's designed to intimidate: The purpose is to make the pro se litigant feel helpless and give up
  • It's unprofessional: Attorneys have ethical duties to the court and to opposing parties, even pro se litigants

"Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere."

Analysis

The Pattern of Intimidation

This email is not an isolated incident. It represents a pattern of behavior by attorneys representing parties seeking to control trust assets:

  • Demeaning language: Calling the pro se litigant an "amateur"
  • Implied threats: Suggesting the "game" is rigged against her
  • Collusion suggestion: Grouping "lawyers and judges" together as "professionals"
  • Dismissiveness: Characterizing legitimate legal filings as "that mess you filed"

When an attorney tells a pro se litigant she is "playing in a game with professionals both lawyers and judges," the implication is clear: the system is not on your side.

This is exactly the kind of intimidation that prevents elderly and disabled beneficiaries from fighting for their rights—and allows trust hijacking schemes to succeed.

The Response

The pro se litigant's response demonstrates the proper reaction to intimidation:

  1. Stay focused on truth: "It's easy when I am on the right side of law and truth."
  2. Document everything: This email exchange is now evidence
  3. Don't be intimidated: Continue pursuing legitimate legal remedies
  4. Remember the bigger picture: Quoting Dr. King reminds us this is about justice, not just one case
