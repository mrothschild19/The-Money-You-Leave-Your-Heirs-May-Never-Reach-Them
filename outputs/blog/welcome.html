Welcome to Trust Hijacking Exposed

April 25, 2026 Miriam Roth Mandel
introduction trust hijacking pennsylvania

This site documents a Pennsylvania family's fight for justice after a stranger walked into Orphans' Court and walked out with control of their family trust.

In Pennsylvania, a stranger can walk into Orphans' Court, file a petition claiming to be a "proposed trustee," and walk out with control of your family's trust assets. No hearing required. No notice to all beneficiaries. Just a judge's signature.

This isn't a hypothetical scenario. This actually happened.

Why This Site Exists

When our family discovered that a stranger had been appointed successor trustee of our parents' trust—without notice to 9 of the 10 beneficiaries—we had a choice: accept it, or fight back through a court system that had already failed us.

We chose to fight.

This site documents that fight. It serves multiple purposes:

  • For journalists: A source for a story that exposes a vulnerability in Pennsylvania's probate system
  • For legislators: Evidence of why reform is needed, with specific proposals
  • For families: A warning and a resource for protecting your own inheritances
  • For the courts: A public record of a family's pursuit of justice

What You'll Find Here

  • Blog posts: Regular updates on the case, legal analysis, and commentary
  • Documents: Court filings, press releases, complaints, and testimony
  • Resources: How to protect your family, where to report exploitation
  • Take Action: How to support reform and help other families

The Core Message

"The money you leave your children may never reach them."

That's not fear-mongering. That's what happened to our family. And unless Pennsylvania's laws and court procedures change, it can happen to any family in the Commonwealth.

We invite you to explore, share, and join us in fighting for reform.

— Miriam Roth Mandel
